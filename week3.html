Week 3 - Industry Attitudes and HR Expectations

Session Details

Date: 15th Feb 2025

Resource Person: Mr. Mahinthan Subenthiran

Designation: Group Manager – Human Resources, NU, MT, MW (SL)

Topic: Industry Attitudes and HR Expectations

Topic Summary

  • Mr. Mahinthan Subenthiran provided a comprehensive overview of the strategic role of human resources (HR) in organizations, focusing on industry expectations and professional attitudes.
  • His insights bridged HR practices with workplace success, emphasizing how IT students can align their skills with industry needs for career growth.

What Was Taught

  • HR is not just a department but a practice focused on managing people with respect and fairness, encompassing recruitment, onboarding, employee relations, learning and development, performance management, compensation, and cultural building.
  • HR distinguishes between equity (fair treatment based on need) and equality (equal treatment for all), fostering inclusive workplaces.
  • HR expects employees to have technical skills, adaptability, critical thinking, communication, professionalism, and digital proficiency.
  • In interviews, HR evaluates skills, cultural fit, and long-term growth potential, emphasizing polished resumes, punctuality, and follow-up etiquette.
  • English proficiency is crucial for global communication, and presentation skills build credibility.
  • Employees must understand employment contracts, labor laws, company policies, and job descriptions to navigate roles effectively.
  • Project proposals involve setting clear goals, defining deliverables, establishing deadlines, and budgeting for structured task management.
  • A quote, “HR is not a job, it’s a practice,” underscored the human-centric nature of HR, while attitude shapes actions, defining professional success.

What Was Learned

  • HR aligns employee performance with organizational goals through managing the employee lifecycle, from recruitment to cultural integration.
  • The distinction between equity and equality deepened my appreciation for fairness and inclusion in professional settings.
  • Crafting effective project proposals is essential for managing tasks efficiently.
  • HR evaluates cultural fit and growth potential in interviews, alongside technical and soft skills.
  • Legal literacy, such as understanding employment contracts and labor laws, is necessary.
  • English proficiency and presentation skills are critical for building a professional presence.
  • A positive attitude and adherence to company policies are vital for workplace success.

What I Plan to Implement in the Future

  • I will cultivate a positive and professional attitude by treating others with respect and fairness, aligning with HR’s emphasis on cultural fit.
  • I’ll apply project proposal principles in academic projects for clarity and structure.
  • I plan to deepen my understanding of HR practices through further reading and observation.
  • I will improve my English proficiency through practice and courses to enhance global communication.
  • I’ll practice presentation skills to confidently share ideas.
  • I will develop a professional LinkedIn profile to showcase skills and engage with industry professionals.
  • I intend to study employment contracts, labor laws, and company policies to prepare for future roles.
  • I aim to align my technical and soft skills with organizational goals to support career growth.
