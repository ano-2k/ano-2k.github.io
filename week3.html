Week 3 - Industry Attitudes and HR Expectations

Session Details

Date: 15th Feb 2025

Resource Person: Mr. Mahinthan Subenthiran

Designation: Group Manager – Human Resources, NU, MT, MW (SL)

Topic: Industry Attitudes and HR Expectations

Topic Summary

Mr. Mahinthan Subenthiran provided a comprehensive overview of the strategic role of human resources (HR) in organizations, focusing on industry expectations and the importance of professional attitudes. His insights bridged HR practices with workplace success, emphasizing how IT students can align their skills with industry needs for career growth.

What Was Taught

Mr. Mahinthan clarified that HR is not just a department but a practice focused on managing people with respect and fairness, encompassing core functions like recruitment, onboarding, employee relations, learning and development, performance management, compensation, and cultural building. He distinguished between equity (fair treatment based on need) and equality (equal treatment for all), highlighting HR’s role in fostering inclusive workplaces. He outlined HR expectations from employees, including technical skills, adaptability, critical thinking, communication, professionalism, and digital proficiency. In interviews, HR evaluates skills, cultural fit, and long-term growth potential, emphasizing professional approaches like polished resumes, punctuality, and follow-up etiquette. He also discussed the importance of English proficiency for global communication and presentation skills for building credibility. Employees must understand employment contracts, labor laws, company policies, and job descriptions to navigate their roles effectively. Additionally, he covered components of a project proposal—setting clear goals, defining deliverables, establishing deadlines, and budgeting—as a tool for structured task management. His quote, “HR is not a job, it’s a practice,” underscored the human-centric nature of HR, while he emphasized that attitude shapes actions, ultimately defining professional success.

What Was Learned

I learned that HR plays a pivotal role in aligning employee performance with organizational goals through managing the employee lifecycle, from recruitment to cultural integration. The distinction between equity and equality deepened my appreciation for fairness and inclusion in professional settings. I gained insights into crafting effective project proposals, which are essential for managing tasks efficiently. The session highlighted the importance of both technical and soft skills in interviews, as HR evaluates cultural fit and growth potential alongside qualifications. I also understood the need for legal literacy, such as understanding employment contracts and labor laws, and the significance of English proficiency and presentation skills in building a professional presence. A positive attitude and adherence to company policies are critical for workplace success.

What I Plan to Implement in the Future

I will focus on cultivating a positive and professional attitude by treating others with respect and fairness, aligning with HR’s emphasis on cultural fit and work ethic. I’ll apply the principles of project proposal development in my academic projects to ensure clarity and structure. To meet industry expectations, I plan to deepen my understanding of HR practices through further reading and observation. Additionally, I will improve my English proficiency through practice and courses to enhance communication with global teams. I’ll practice presentation skills to confidently share ideas and develop a professional LinkedIn profile to showcase my skills and engage with industry professionals. I also intend to study employment contracts, labor laws, and company policies to prepare for future roles. In the long term, I aim to align my technical and soft skills with organizational goals to support career growth.
