Week 5 - Job Role – UI/UX Engineering

Session Details

Date: 8th Mar 2025

Resource Person: Mr. Gobinthiran Kulendran

Designation: CEO, Huex

Topic: Job Role – UI/UX Engineering

Topic Summary

Mr. Gobinthiran Kulendran delivered an insightful session on UI/UX engineering, explaining its critical role in creating user-friendly digital products that enhance user retention and drive business revenue. He highlighted its competitive edge in the tech industry and the growing global demand for UI/UX professionals, particularly in Sri Lanka and international markets.

What Was Taught

Mr. Gobinthiran explained the distinction between User Interface (UI), which focuses on a product’s look and interactivity, and User Experience (UX), which enhances usability, accessibility, and user satisfaction. He detailed the Design Thinking process—empathize, define, ideate, prototype, test, implement—as the most common UI/UX framework, alongside other methodologies like Double Diamond, Lean UX, and Google’s Design Sprint for proof-of-concept development. He introduced tools such as Figma, Adobe XD, and Balsamiq for design and wireframing, and Hotjar and Clarity for user research and analytics. Essential skills for UI/UX designers included psychology to understand user behavior, critical thinking for solving design problems, market research for analyzing trends, communication for defending design decisions, empathy for user-centric design, and basic coding knowledge (HTML, CSS, JavaScript) for collaboration with developers. He outlined career roles like UI Designer, UX Designer, Product Designer, and UX Researcher, noting their growing demand in Sri Lanka (startups, banking, e-commerce) and globally (USA, UK, Middle East). He emphasized the importance of continuous learning, freelancing opportunities on platforms like Upwork and Fiverr, and running a UX agency. The session underscored user-centered design principles and the need for a strong portfolio and LinkedIn presence to showcase skills.

What Was Learned

I gained a comprehensive understanding of the UI/UX design process, particularly the iterative Design Thinking framework, and its profound impact on user satisfaction and business success. The session clarified that UI/UX is not just about aesthetics but about solving user problems through empathy and research. I learned about the diverse tools and skills required, from Figma for prototyping to psychology for understanding users, and the variety of career paths available, including freelancing and agency work. The importance of building a portfolio with 3-5 detailed projects and optimizing my LinkedIn profile for networking was a key takeaway. I also recognized the global demand for UI/UX professionals and the need for continuous learning to stay competitive in this dynamic field.

What I Plan to Implement in the Future

I will explore online platforms like Udemy, Coursera (Google UX Design Certificate), and the Interaction Design Foundation to learn UI/UX design principles and tools. I plan to practice wireframing and prototyping using Figma and Balsamiq to enhance my design skills. I’ll build a portfolio showcasing 3-5 projects with detailed process breakdowns, hosted on platforms like Behance or a personal website, and optimize my LinkedIn profile by highlighting UI/UX skills, projects, and certifications. I intend to read recommended books like "The Design of Everyday Things" by Donald A. Norman to deepen my understanding of design principles. I’ll also explore freelancing opportunities on platforms like Upwork to gain practical experience. In the long term, I aim to incorporate user-centered design principles into my projects to create intuitive, impactful solutions and pursue a career in UI/UX, potentially as a UX Designer or Product Designer.
